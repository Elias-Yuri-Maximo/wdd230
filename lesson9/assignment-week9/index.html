Abnormally Dry Weather in Idaho, says William Macbeth

Author: Jamnes Nortonson

  Dry weather for the Treasure Valley Wednesday with light winds and near seasonal temperatures. High clouds will continue to stream into the region from the west into the afternoon and then move south. Highs will reach the low 50's for the valley, mid to upper 30's for the mountain valleys.
  On Thursday, another surge of moisture will bring additional valley rain and high elevation snow. Moisture will arrive very early Thursday morning in southwest Idaho and bring snow to mountain valleys initially, likely transitioning over to a rain/snow mix by late morning and early afternoon. Snow levels will hover above 3,500 feet with minor accumulations expected. Rain in the Treasure Valley will taper off throughout the afternoon, roughly 0.10-0.30" of rain is expected.
  High pressure will push the storm track just north of the Treasure Valley for the weekend. Expect mostly cloudy skies Saturday and Sunday. Temperatures will likely hover in the upper 50's in the valley, mid 40's in the mountain valleys. The mountain areas to the north, however, will continue to see additional rain and snow showers into the weekend. Snow accumulations will likely remain above 6,000 feet with the best chances of precipitation falling on Friday and Saturday. The long-range forecast shows another storm could also be in the works for Tuesday of next week.
